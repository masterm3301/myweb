# Travel: A Bridge to Our True Selves


Traveling alone — or with someone who loves adventure and discovering new things — is an experience every person should have at least once in their life.
A lot of people dream of traveling the world, but when you ask them about it, they usually say it’s impossible. They talk about work, responsibilities, and commitments. The truth is, traveling is mostly about courage. Even if you have almost nothing, you can still travel. All you need is two feet to explore places in your own country. And if you can afford a plane ticket to a nearby country, then go. Visit its cities. Experience its culture.
When you travel, you meet new people from all walks of life. You try new things, face new challenges, and see the world through different eyes. Most importantly, you grow. You become a better version of yourself.
I once met a British Muslim who shared something beautiful about travel. We were talking about religion when he mentioned that Buddhists travel to experience letting go. He said:
“Travel teaches you that everyone in your life will eventually leave — sooner or later. People come and go. They leave places, they leave things, and they leave people behind. That’s part of life. When you let go of what’s outside, you get closer to what’s inside. You begin to understand yourself better… and maybe even find something greater.”
That’s why, in Morocco and in many Arab countries in the past — especially in small villages far from the cities — people used to treat every traveler as a guest of God. They believed that anyone on the road was under God’s protection. Travelers were welcomed warmly, and special houses were built just to host them.
Here’s the heart of it:
Travel is a bridge back to yourself.
If you’ve lost your way, travel to find it again.
There are places that feel like they’ve been waiting for you. The moment you arrive, your loneliness disappears — and suddenly, it feels like home.
Some places are made for the body. Others are made for the soul. Take care of your soul, my friend. The body will fade one day, but the soul lives on.
Travel. Open the windows of your heart to the sky. The stars will lean in, curious to hear your story — the story of a heart that’s been tired, distracted, and proud.
Travel until your body is tired and your soul feels light again. Every day you don’t travel is a day that doesn’t truly belong to your life.
Travel until the laziness leaves your mind and the boredom leaves your heart.
